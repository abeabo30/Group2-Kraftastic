Cornell Note-Taking Method

by: Walter Pauk

The Cornell Note-Taking Method is a systematic approach to capturing and organizing lecture or reading notes. It provides a structured format that encourages active engagement with the material, promotes comprehension, and facilitates review and retention. The method involves dividing your note page into specific sections for main points, supporting details, and summarization, allowing for efficient study and retrieval of information.

Supplies Needed:

  • Notebook or loose-leaf paper
  • Pen or pencil
  • Ruler or straight edge (optional)
  • Highlighters or colored pens (optional)

Step 1: Set Up Your Page

Begin by dividing your paper into three sections: a narrow column on the left (about 2.5 inches wide), a larger area on the right for notes (about 6 inches wide), and a section at the bottom for summarizing (2 inches tall). You can use a ruler or fold your paper to create these sections neatly.

Step 2: Record Main Points

During a lecture or while reading, jot down main points and key ideas in the right-hand section of your page. Focus on capturing the most important information, using concise phrases or bullet points to keep your notes organized.

Step 3: Capture Supporting Details

As you listen to the lecture or read the text, use the larger section to record supporting details, examples, explanations, or relevant facts that elaborate on the main points. Be sure to leave enough space between each point to allow for later additions or annotations.

Step 4: Use the Cue Column

After the lecture or reading, review your notes and use the narrow column on the left (the cue column) to create cues or questions based on the main points and supporting details. These cues will serve as prompts for later review and self-testing.

Step 5: Summarize at the Bottom

At the bottom of the page, write a brief summary of the entire page of notes. This summary should encapsulate the main points and key takeaways in your own words, reinforcing your understanding of the material.

Step 6: Review and Reinforce

Once your notes are complete, review them regularly to reinforce your understanding and retention of the material. Use the cues in the left-hand column to quiz yourself on the content, covering the notes in the right-hand column as you recall the information.

Step 7: Highlight or Annotate

To further enhance your notes, consider using highlighters or colored pens to emphasize important concepts, key terms, or connections between ideas. You can also add annotations or comments in the margins to provide additional context or clarification.

Step 8: Create a Summary Page

As you accumulate more Cornell notes, consider creating a summary page or index to organize and review your notes more efficiently. This can help you identify recurring themes, connections, or areas where you may need further review.

Step 9: Adapt to Your Needs

The Cornell Note-Taking Method is flexible and can be adapted to suit your individual learning style and preferences. Feel free to modify the layout or incorporate additional sections to better meet your study needs.

Step 10: Practice Consistently

Like any study method, mastering the Cornell Note-Taking Method takes practice. Experiment with different techniques and strategies to find what works best for you, and make a habit of using Cornell notes consistently to maximize their effectiveness in your learning and academic success.
