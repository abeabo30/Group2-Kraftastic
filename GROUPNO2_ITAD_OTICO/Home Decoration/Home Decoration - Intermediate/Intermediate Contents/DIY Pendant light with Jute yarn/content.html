DIY Pendant Light with Jute Yarn

by: cuckoo4design

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • jute yarn (4 rolls)
  • white pendant light kit/lighting kit
  • 2 thrifted vintage lampshades that have a cage frame ($2.99 and $4.99), the one I used had an 18″ bottom radius
  • soldering iron
  • water soluble flux and lead free solder
  • finer grit sandpaper
  • wire cutter
  • small artist brush
  • scissors
  • small paper clip
  • plastic pipe (plumbing section)
  • glass pendant dome for bulb socket (optional)


A DIY pendant light with jute yarn is a project where you create a hanging light fixture using jute yarn as the primary material. This involves wrapping, weaving, or covering a frame or structure with jute yarn to form the outer shell of the pendant light. The process typically includes constructing or repurposing a wire frame or pendant light kit, then wrapping or weaving jute yarn around it to create a textured and visually appealing surface. The jute yarn adds a natural and rustic touch to the pendant light, making it suitable for various interior design styles such as bohemian, coastal, or farmhouse. This DIY project offers flexibility for customization in terms of shape, size, and design, allowing individuals to create unique and personalized lighting fixtures for their homes.

STEP 1: Cutting

Start out by removing the fabric from the lampshade frames. 

Step 2:

Cut the rods from the one lampshade frame (that you aren’t using) so you can use them as parts for the soldering part. I cut mine to 5 inches long.

Step 3:

Sand the gold layer off of the macrame ring with sandpaper. 

Step 4:

Brush the flux onto the areas where you want to attach the rod parts.

Step 5:

Solder 4 of the rods onto the macrame hoop from both sides by melting and dripping the solder onto the connection. Keep brushing on flux before soldering as you go.

Step 6:

Time to attach the macrame ring with rods to the top of the lampshade cage frame.

Step 7:

I used duct tape which I each removed as I went around the perimeter of the frame to solder it all together.

Step 8:

I liked using this broad tip to solder the connections.

Step 9:

Here you can see the finished cage for the pendant light before wrapping it with jute.

Step 10:

Start by attaching the jute yarn to the center lampshade ring which you can untie later when you are finished.

Step 11:

Start wrapping the yarn around the pendant frame but not to tight to make sure the frame doesn’t collapse. 

Step 12:

When you get to a part where the metal rods are, I just used a small bundle of yarn cut off from the larger bundle so it would fit through the narrow openings. 

Step 12:

When Tying a new piece of yarn to the wrapped areas because you ran out of yarn, don’t tie it in the center of the pendant because that will be visible later when the light shines through.

Step 14:

Tie the yarn together at the middle ring instead as shown. This are isn’t visible when the light shines through.

Step 15:

When you wrapped the entire shade with jute yarn, untie the yarn from the center ring piece and tie it to the other piece of yarn for a nice finish. 

Step 16:

Cut the plastic rod with a handsaw. I cut mine to 7 inches but it depends on what size of lampshade you are using and how far you want the bulb to hang down.

Step 17:

I bought a cheap glass pendant cover for safety reasons because I want to make sure that the hot lightbulb never has a chance to touch the yarn should it ever move. 

Step 18:

Remove the screw at the top of the metal lid so you can thread the plastic rod onto the electrical cord. 

Step 19:

Now thread the electric cord through the lampshade hole so the entire pendant rests on the plastic rod. Then reassemble the cord and screw through the top metal part the way it was when you bought the pendant light kit.

Step 20:

All that’s left to do is to hang the new jute pendant light. I have a bit of experience with hanging new lighting and feel comfortable doing it. If you are new to it you might want to hire an electrician. It is honestly pretty simple to do though.
