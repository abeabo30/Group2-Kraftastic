Gallery Wall of  Memories

by: theturquoisehome

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • a deep frame
  • one iconic picture, prefarebly a landscape picture and it’s ideally a photograph you took yourself on the trip
  • few little items such as banknotes and coins, tourist map, business card of your hotel/restaurant, some sand, sea shells, whatever item reminds you. Be creative! For example you can lookout for a miniature scooter if at the place you’ve been to, you rented a scooter etc.
  • A4 colour print
  • superglue
  • Photoshop or a similar program to create the background

Create a charming gallery wall filled with your favorite photos, artwork, and mementos to showcase cherished memories and add personality to your space. Start by gathering an assortment of frames in different sizes, shapes, and colors from thrift stores or discount retailers. Arrange the frames on the floor to experiment with different layouts until you find one that you love. Then, using nails or adhesive hooks, hang the frames on your wall, making sure they're evenly spaced and aligned. Mix and match photos, prints, and other keepsakes to tell your unique story and create a focal point that reflects your personality and style.

STEP 1:

Open Photoshop and create your Background Layer with the dimensions: width: 23 cm, height:23cm and a resolution of 300dpi

STEP 2:

Use the Text-Tool to write the name of the destination you’ve been to.

STEP 3:

Use a font that makes the letters big and bold and thick so you can have as much of your photograph exposed. For example Arial is a great font to use. If the name of the destination is to wide for the frame, continue with step 4, otherwise skip it and continue with step 5.

STEP 4:

Use the text-tool to reduce the spacing of the font. Here’s how: Window->paragraph->text tool tab opens->change the number of the spacing until your text fits in the frame.

STEP 5:

Select the Text: Go with the cursor on the layer of your text and click while holding at the same time command on your keyboard. Now your text should be outlined with a dashed line.

STEP 6:

Create a new layer and invert the selection by clicking command+shift+I, this should result in an inverted selection: Now the text and your frame are outlined with a dashed line.

STEP 7:

Use the fill-tool to colour everything but your text in a colour of your choice. As you can see in my example, I always used a white background.

STEP 8:

Now you can add another layer and insert your selected picture. By placing your photograph between your text layer and the inverted selection layer, your text should be filled up with your chosen photograph.
