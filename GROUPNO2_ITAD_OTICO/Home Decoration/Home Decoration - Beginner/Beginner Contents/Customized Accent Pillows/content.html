Customized Accent Pillows

by: theturquoisehome

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Fabric of your choice. I bought a yard of my favorite fabric for the front of the pillow and a yard of neutral fabric for the back of my pillow. I made 2 matching pillows.
  • Fabric tape measure or transparent ruler(optional: cutting mat)
  • Scissors or rotary cutter
  • Basic sewing machine

Add a pop of color and personality to your living room or bedroom with custom-made accent pillows. Start by selecting fabric in colors and patterns that complement your decor style. Cut the fabric into squares or rectangles, then sew or use fabric glue to assemble the pillow covers. Fill the covers with pillow inserts or stuffing, and add embellishments like pom-poms, tassels, or decorative buttons for extra flair. Mix and match different fabrics and textures to create a dynamic look that reflects your individual taste. These DIY accent pillows are an easy way to refresh your space and make it feel cozy and inviting.

Step 1: Decide on the Size

Decide on the Size - I first decided what size to make my pillows. I had Euro inserts, which are 26 x 26 inches. And I wanted the pillows to be somewhat plump. So, I decided to cut the fabric at 26 x 26, so that after I sewed it all up, it would be a little smaller and the insert would fill it up.

Step 2: Measure your Fabric

Measure your Fabric - I only ordered 1 yard of the Braemore fabric, and decided to use some less expensive fabric for the backs. (I think it was just an off white canvas material.) Since the floral pattern repeated twice on one yard, I cut out a “frame” from some wrapping paper.

Step 3: Cut Your Fabric

Cut Your Fabric - The thing that was really nice about the wrapping paper method is that it helped me picture the pattern I wanted on the front of the pillow. And once I cut out the first square, I just placed it over the other half of the fabric and it was easy to cut out the second square.I cut out two pieces of the back fabric for each pillow. One piece was 26 x 20 inches and the other piece was 26 x 16 inches. This left plenty of room for the two pieces to overlap on the back and to have a generous hem allowance.

Step 4: Iron Your Fabric

Iron Your Fabric - I then ironed all of the pieces of fabric. This isn’t necessary, but it will make your sewing job so much easier if you do!

Step 5: Sew the Edges

Sew the Edges - I hate that I didn’t take pictures of all the steps to sew, but I took a picture of a finished envelope pillow and I’ll try to show you what I did with arrows and such. First, you want to hem the two sides of your back pieces. These are the edges that will come together in the back to create your envelope. I folded the hem down 1 inch and ironed it. Then I folded it again and ironed it again, and then hemmed it. You can see how I’m ironing the back piece in the above picture. Once your two back pieces are hemmed on one long side, you’ll want to put your front piece FACING UP toward you. Then you’ll place the two back pieces on top, making sure they are all squared up. The two hemmed edges should be facing up at you. Pin each side in a few places and then sew the four sides, leaving a 1-inch allowance.

Step 6: Cut the Corners Off

Cut the Corners Off - Now cut the excess fabric off of each corner, making sure to not cut your stitch. This will keep the corners from being bunchy when you turn the pillow inside out.

Step 7:

Turn your pillow inside out, stuff the insert inside and fluff. You now have your very own pillow cover, which was super easy to make.
