DIY Hamster Playground with Cardboard Boxes

by: Wendy Tremayne

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • PVC pipe (diameter and length depending on the size of your rabbit and the amount of hay needed)
  • PVC pipe end caps (2)
  • PVC pipe elbows (2)
  • PVC pipe T-joint (1)
  • PVC pipe adhesive (optional)
  • Saw or PVC pipe cutter
  • Sandpaper (optional)

A DIY hamster playground with cardboard boxes is a fun and stimulating environment that provides your furry friend with opportunities for exploration, exercise, and enrichment. Hamsters are curious creatures that love to climb, tunnel, and explore new spaces, and a cardboard box playground offers them a safe and customizable area to do so. By repurposing cardboard boxes and adding various accessories, you can create a dynamic and interactive playground that will keep your hamster entertained for hours.

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

Gather Your Materials Collect cardboard boxes of different sizes and shapes. Ensure they are clean and free from any tape or stickers.

Step 2: Plan Your Playground

Plan Your Playground Decide on the layout and design of your hamster playground. Consider incorporating tunnels, ramps, platforms, and hiding spots to provide variety and stimulation.

Step 3: Cut Openings and Passageways

Cut Openings and Passageways Use scissors or a utility knife to cut openings in the cardboard boxes to create tunnels and entrances. You can also cut holes in the sides of the boxes to connect them together.

Step 4: Assemble the Playground

Assemble the Playground Arrange the cardboard boxes in an interesting and interconnected layout, stacking them to create multiple levels if desired. Use non-toxic glue or tape to secure the boxes together and create stable structures.

Step 5: Add Accessories

Add Accessories Enhance the playground with additional accessories such as tunnels, ramps, and platforms. You can create ramps by cutting strips of cardboard and attaching them to the boxes with glue or tape. Tunnels can be made from rolled-up cardboard or purchased from pet stores.

Step 6: Decorate (Optional)

Decorate (Optional) Use non-toxic markers or paint to decorate the cardboard boxes and add visual interest to the playground. Get creative and personalize the design to match your hamster's personality or your home decor.

Step 7: Fill with Bedding Line

Fill with Bedding Line the bottom of the cardboard boxes with bedding material such as shredded paper or aspen shavings. This will provide a comfortable and cozy substrate for your hamster to burrow and nest in.

Step 7: Introduce Your Hamster

Introduce Your Hamster Once the playground is assembled and decorated, introduce your hamster to their new environment. Allow them to explore and interact with the various features at their own pace.

Step 8: Rotate and Refresh

Rotate and Refresh Periodically rotate and refresh the toys and accessories in the playground to keep your hamster engaged and prevent boredom. You can also hide treats or snacks throughout the playground to encourage foraging and exploration.
