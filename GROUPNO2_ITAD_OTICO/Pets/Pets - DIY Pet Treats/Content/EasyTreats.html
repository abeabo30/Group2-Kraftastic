Easy Treats

by: Anna Hasselbring




Beginner Friendly Pet Treats

DIY pet treats are a fantastic way to pamper your furry friend while ensuring they consume wholesome, nutritious snacks. Crafting homemade treats allows you to control the ingredients, avoiding additives and preservatives commonly found in commercial pet treats. Easy DIY pet treats require minimal ingredients and preparation, making them accessible for pet owners of all skill levels. By following simple recipes and using basic kitchen equipment, you can whip up delicious treats that your pet will adore.

General Tips

  1. Choose Simple Recipes Opt for recipes with minimal ingredients and straightforward instructions. Look for recipes that utilize common pantry staples and require little to no cooking or baking.
  2. Use Pet-Safe Ingredients Prioritize your pet's health and safety by selecting ingredients that are safe for their consumption. Common pet-friendly ingredients include plain yogurt, peanut butter (free of xylitol), canned pumpkin, unsweetened applesauce, and lean meats.
  3. Consider Your Pet's Preferences Tailor your treat recipes to suit your pet's tastes and dietary needs. If your pet has any allergies or sensitivities, choose ingredients that won't trigger adverse reactions.
  4. Portion Control Treats should be given in moderation to prevent overfeeding and maintain a healthy weight for your pet. Consider the size of your pet and adjust treat portions accordingly
  5. Storage Properly store homemade treats in airtight containers or resealable bags to maintain freshness and prevent spoilage. Store treats in the refrigerator if they contain perishable ingredients.
  6. Experiment with Shapes Get creative with shaping your treats to make them more appealing to your pet. Use cookie cutters to create fun shapes like hearts, stars, or bones.

Things You Will Need

  1. Basic Ingredients Common ingredients for easy DIY pet treats include flour (such as whole wheat or oat flour), eggs, peanut butter, canned pumpkin, and plain yogurt.
  2. Mixing Bowl and Utensils Gather a mixing bowl, spoon, and spatula for mixing and shaping the treats.
  3. Cookie Cutter (Optional) If you want to shape your treats into fun shapes, have a cookie cutter on hand. Alternatively, you can simply roll the dough into balls or use a knife to cut it into squares.
  4. Baking Sheet Use a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat for baking treats in the oven.
  5. Cooling Rack Allow baked treats to cool completely on a wire cooling rack before serving or storing.
  6. Storage Containers Have airtight containers or resealable bags ready for storing the treats once they're cooked.
