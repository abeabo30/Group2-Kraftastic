DIY Cat Tree House Tower

by: Natalie Fee



MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Wooden boards or plywood sheets (for platforms and shelves)
  • Carpet or sisal rope (for covering surfaces)
  • Wooden posts or PVC pipes (for support)
  • Nails, screws, and brackets
  • Drill and drill bits
  • Saw
  • Staple gun and staples
  • Non-toxic wood glue
  • Optional: decorative fabric or cushions for added comfort

A cat tree house tower is an exciting and adventurous addition to your feline friend's environment, providing them with vertical space to climb, perch, and explore. Cats love to climb and survey their surroundings from elevated positions, and a treehouse tower offers them the perfect opportunity to satisfy their natural instincts. By making your own cat treehouse tower, you can customize it to fit your cat's preferences and your home decor, while also providing a stimulating and enriching environment for your furry friend.

Step 1: Plan Your Design

Plan Your Design Decide on the size and layout of your cat treehouse tower based on the available space in your home and your cat's preferences. Sketch out a design that includes platforms, shelves, scratching posts, and any other features you'd like to include.

Step 2: Cut the Wood

Cut the Wood Use a saw to cut the wooden boards or plywood sheets into the desired shapes and sizes for the platforms and shelves. Sand down any rough edges to prevent splinters.

Step 3: Cover Surfaces

Cover Surfaces If desired, cover the surfaces of the platforms and shelves with carpet or sisal rope to provide traction and encourage scratching. Use a staple gun to secure the carpet or rope in place, ensuring it's taut and securely attached.

Step 4: Assemble the Structure

Assemble the StructureÂ  Assemble the wooden posts or PVC pipes to create the vertical supports for the tower. Use nails, screws, and brackets to secure the posts in place, ensuring they are sturdy and stable.

Step 5: Attach Platforms

Attach Platforms and Shelves Attach the platforms and shelves to the vertical supports using nails, screws, and wood glue. Place them at varying heights to create opportunities for climbing and perching.

Step 6: Add Scratching

Add Scratching Posts Attach sisal rope or carpet-wrapped posts to the sides of the tower to provide scratching surfaces for your cat. Secure the posts in place with nails, screws, and brackets, ensuring they are tall enough for your cat to stretch and scratch.

Step 7: Optional: Add Decorative Elements

Optional: Add Decorative Elements If desired, add decorative fabric or cushions to the platforms and shelves for added comfort and style. You can also incorporate toys or hanging ropes for additional enrichment.

Step 8: Secure to Wall (Optional)

Secure to Wall (Optional) For added stability, consider securing the cat treehouse tower to the wall using brackets or anchors. This will prevent it from tipping over during vigorous play.

Step 9: Introduce to Your Cat

Introduce to Your Cat Once the cat treehouse tower is assembled and securely in place, introduce it to your cat and encourage them to explore and climb. Use treats or toys to entice them to use the tower, and provide praise and rewards for positive behavior.
