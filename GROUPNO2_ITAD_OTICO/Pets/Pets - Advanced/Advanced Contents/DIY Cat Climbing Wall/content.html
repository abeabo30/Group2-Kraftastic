DIY Cat Climbing Wall

by: Lauren Singer

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Wooden boards or plywood sheets
  • Wall brackets or shelf brackets
  • Wood screws
  • Carpet or sisal rope
  • Non-toxic paint or stain (optional)
  • Non-toxic glue or staple gun
  • Drill and drill bits
  • Saw
  • Level
  • Stud finder (if attaching to wall studs)

A DIY cat climbing wall is an excellent way to provide your feline friend with vertical space to explore, climb, and play. Cats naturally enjoy climbing and perching in high places, and a climbing wall allows them to indulge in their instinctive behaviors while also providing exercise and mental stimulation. By creating your own cat climbing wall, you can customize it to suit your cat's preferences and your home decor, while also saving money compared to store-bought options.

Step 1: Plan Your Design

Plan Your Design Decide on the size and layout of your cat climbing wall based on the available space in your home and your cat's preferences. Sketch out a design that includes platforms, shelves, scratching surfaces, and any other features you'd like to include.

STEP 2: Cut the Wood

Cut the Wood Use a saw to cut the wooden boards or plywood sheets into the desired shapes and sizes for the platforms and shelves. Sand down any rough edges to prevent splinters.

STEP 3: Attach Wall Brackets

Attach Wall Brackets Use wall brackets or shelf brackets to attach the wooden platforms and shelves to the wall. Place the brackets at varying heights to create opportunities for climbing and perching. Use a level to ensure the platforms are straight and level.

STEP 4: Secure the Platforms

Secure the Platforms Once the brackets are in place, attach the wooden platforms and shelves to the brackets using wood screws. Make sure they are securely fastened and can support the weight of your cat.

STEP 5: Cover Surfaces

Cover Surfaces If desired, cover the surfaces of the platforms and shelves with carpet or sisal rope to provide traction and encourage scratching. Use non-toxic glue or a staple gun to secure the carpet or rope in place, ensuring it's taut and securely attached.

STEP 6: Add Decorative Elements

Add Decorative Elements If desired, paint or stain the wooden platforms and shelves to match your home decor. You can also add decorative elements such as trim or molding to enhance the aesthetic appeal of the climbing wall.

STEP 7: Install Scratching Surfaces

Install Scratching Surfaces Attach sisal rope or carpet-wrapped posts to the sides of the climbing wall to provide scratching surfaces for your cat. Secure the posts in place with wood screws and ensure they are tall enough for your cat to stretch and scratch.
