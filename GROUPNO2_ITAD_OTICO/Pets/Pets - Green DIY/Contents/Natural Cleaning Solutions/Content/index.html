Natural Cleaning Solutions

by: Keri Sanders




Natural Cleaning Solutions

Making a dog chew toy out of old T-shirts is a fun and sustainable way to repurpose materials while providing your furry friend with an engaging and durable toy. Dogs love to chew, and T-shirt material can offer both texture and durability for their chewing needs. By braiding or knotting the T-shirt fabric, you create a sturdy toy that can withstand a lot of chewing and tugging. This DIY project not only benefits your pet but also helps reduce textile waste by giving new life to old clothing.


General Tips:


  1. Choose suitable t-shirts select old t-shirts made from durable fabric, preferably cotton, as it's safe for dogs to chew on and won't easily fray or break apart.

  2. Wash and cut t-shirts wash the t-shirts thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris. Then, cut the T-shirts into strips of equal width. The width of the strips will determine the size of the finished toy; thinner strips create a tighter braid, while thicker strips result in a larger, chunkier toy.

  3. Braiding or knotting technique there are several techniques you can use to turn the t-shirt strips into a chew toy. Braiding is a popular option, where you simply braid the strips together tightly. Another method is knotting, where you tie the strips together in knots along the length of the toy. Experiment with different techniques to see which your dog prefers.

  4. Add reinforcement for added durability, you can incorporate additional materials into the toy, such as nylon rope or rubber strips. These materials can help strengthen the toy and make it last longer, especially for dogs with strong chewing habits.

  5. Supervise your dog while these DIY chew toys are designed to be safe for dogs, it's essential to supervise your pet during playtime to ensure they don't ingest any loose threads or fabric. Replace the toy if it starts to show signs of wear and tear to prevent choking hazards.

  6. Washable design choose a design that is easy to clean, as the toy will inevitably get dirty with regular use. Machine washable materials are ideal for maintaining hygiene.

Things You Will Need:


  1. Old T-shirts: Choose T-shirts made from durable, non-toxic fabric, preferably cotton.

  2. Scissors: For cutting the T-shirts into strips.

  3. Optional: Reinforcement Materials Nylon rope, rubber strips, or other durable materials to strengthen the toy.

  4. Washing Machine: For washing the T-shirts before cutting and assembling the toy.
