DIY Cat Scratching Post with Sisal Rope

by: Shawn Galloway

A cat scratching post with sisal rope is a practical and satisfying project that provides your feline friend with an outlet for their natural scratching instincts. Sisal rope is a durable and attractive material that cats love to sink their claws into, making it ideal for creating a scratching surface. By making your own scratching post, you can customize the size and style to suit your cat's preferences and your home decor, while also saving money compared to store-bought options.

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Wooden post or sturdy cardboard tube (height and diameter depending on your preference)
  • Sisal rope (enough to cover the length of the post)
  • Hot glue gun and glue sticks
  • Scissors
  • Optional: decorative fabric, catnip spray

STEP 1: Prepare the Post

Prepare the Post If you're using a wooden post, ensure it's smooth and free from any splinters. If using a cardboard tube, make sure it's sturdy enough to withstand your cat's scratching. You may need to trim the height of the post to your desired size using a saw.

STEP 2: Start Wrapping

Start Wrapping with Sisal Rope Begin by securing the end of the sisal rope to the top of the post using hot glue. Hold it in place until the glue dries to ensure it's securely attached. Then, start wrapping the rope tightly around the post, overlapping each layer slightly to ensure full coverage.

STEP 3: Apply Glue

Apply Glue as You Go As you wrap the sisal rope around the post, periodically apply hot glue to the post to keep the rope in place. Be careful not to use too much glue, as it can create lumps or bumps in the rope. Continue wrapping and gluing until you reach the bottom of the post.

STEP 4: Finish Wrapping

Finish Wrapping Once you reach the bottom of the post, cut the sisal rope and secure the end with hot glue. Press it firmly to ensure it's well-attached and won't come loose. Trim any excess rope if necessary.

STEP 5: Optional: Add Decorative Fabric

Optional: Add Decorative Fabric If you'd like to add a decorative touch to your scratching post, you can wrap the base of the post with decorative fabric or felt. Use hot glue to secure the fabric in place and trim any excess.

STEP 6: Sprinkle with Catnip (Optional)

Sprinkle with Catnip (Optional) To entice your cat to use the scratching post, you can sprinkle it with catnip or spray it with catnip spray. This will attract your cat to the post and encourage them to start scratching.

STEP 7: Introduce to Your Cat

. Introduce to Your Cat the scratching post in an area where your cat likes to scratch, such as near their favorite sleeping spot or by a window. Encourage your cat to use the post by gently guiding their paws to it and rewarding them with treats or praise when they scratch on it.
