Woven Fabric

by: Sarah Howard

Woven clothing refers to garments made from fabric created by interlacing yarns or threads at right angles. Unlike knitted fabric, which is created by interlocking loops of yarn, woven fabric is produced on a loom. Woven clothing encompasses a wide range of styles, from everyday basics like shirts, pants, and dresses to more intricate pieces like jackets, skirts, and formal attire.

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Woven fabric (yardage depends on the clothing piece)
  • Sewing machine or needle and thread
  • Fabric scissors
  • Pins
  • Measuring tape
  • Iron
  • Clothing pattern (you can find free patterns online or create your own)

Step 1: Choose Your Fabric and Pattern

Select the woven fabric you want to use for your clothing piece. Consider the weight, drape, and pattern of the fabric, depending on the style of clothing you're making. Then, find a clothing pattern that matches your desired style and size.

Step 2: Cut Out Your Fabric Pieces

Using your chosen pattern, carefully cut out the fabric pieces needed for your clothing item. Be sure to follow the cutting layout provided with the pattern to ensure that you cut the pieces correctly and efficiently.

Step 3: Prepare Your Fabric

Before sewing, it's a good idea to press your fabric pieces with an iron to remove any wrinkles and ensure smooth sewing. Follow the fabric care instructions to avoid damaging the fabric.

Step 4: Pin and Sew the Pieces Together

Begin by pinning the fabric pieces together with the right sides facing each other, following the instructions provided with your pattern. Use sewing pins to secure the pieces in place.

Using a sewing machine or needle and thread, sew along the pinned edges, following the seam allowances specified in the pattern instructions. Be sure to backstitch at the beginning and end of each seam to secure the stitches.

Step 5: Finish Raw Edges

After sewing each seam, trim any excess fabric and finish the raw edges to prevent fraying. You can use techniques like zigzag stitching, serging, or using pinking shears to finish the edges neatly.

Step 6: Press Seams

Once all the pieces are sewn together, press the seams with an iron to set the stitches and give the garment a professional finish. Press the seams open or to one side, depending on the instructions provided with your pattern.

Step 7: Add Fastenings and Closures

If your clothing item requires fastenings or closures, such as buttons, zippers, or snaps, follow the pattern instructions to add them to the garment. Be sure to sew them securely to ensure they hold up during wear.

Step 8: Finish and Hem

Once you're satisfied with the fit, finish any remaining details, such as hemming the bottom edge, sleeves, and neckline. Press the finished garment one final time for a polished look
