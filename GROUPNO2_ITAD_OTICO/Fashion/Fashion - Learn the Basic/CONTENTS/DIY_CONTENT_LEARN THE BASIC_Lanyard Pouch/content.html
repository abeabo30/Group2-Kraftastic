Lanyard Pouch

by: Torie and Ren

Crafting a lanyard pouch from fabric is a straightforward yet rewarding DIY endeavor. Start by cutting fabric pieces for the pouch body and lining, then sew them together with a zipper at the top for closure. Fold the pouch in half, stitch the bottom, and turn it right side out. Attach a fabric loop for the lanyard near the zipper, completing it with a clip or keyring.

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Fabric of your choice (cotton, canvas, or other sturdy material)
  • Coordinating thread
  • Lanyard hardware (lobster clasp and keyring)
  • Scissors
  • Sewing machine or needle and thread
  • Iron and ironing board
  • Pins
  • Optional: fusible interfacing for added structure

Step 1: Cut Fabric Pieces

Decide on the dimensions of your lanyard pouch. You'll need two pieces of fabric: one for the main body of the pouch and one for the pocket. Cut these pieces according to your desired size. You can use a ruler or template to ensure accurate measurements.

Step 2: Prepare Pocket (Optional)

If you want to add a pocket to your lanyard pouch, cut an additional piece of fabric for the pocket. Fold the edges of the pocket fabric inward and press with an iron to create clean edges. Sew around the edges of the pocket to secure them in place.

Step 3: Attach Pocket (Optional)

Place the pocket fabric onto one of the main fabric pieces, positioning it where you want it to be on the pouch. Pin the pocket in place, then sew around the sides and bottom of the pocket to attach it to the main fabric piece.

Step 4: Fold and Press

Fold the main fabric piece in half with right sides facing together, aligning the edges. Press the fold with an iron to create a crease. This fold will become the top edge of the pouch.

Step 5: Sew Side Seams

1. With the main fabric piece still folded in half, sew along the side edges, leaving the top (folded) edge open. Backstitch at the beginning and end of each seam to secure the stitches.

Step 6: Trim Excess Fabric

Trim any excess fabric from the seam allowances to reduce bulk and create clean edges. Be careful not to cut into the stitches.

Step 7: Turn Right Side Out

Turn the pouch right side out by pulling it through the open top edge. Use a turning tool or the blunt end of a pencil to push out the corners and smooth the edges.

Step 8: Attach Lanyard Hardware

Cut a piece of fabric or ribbon for the lanyard strap, ensuring it's long enough to fit comfortably around your neck. Attach one end of the strap to the top edge of the pouch using a lobster clasp and keyring. Sew the strap securely in place.

Step 9: Finish and Enjoy

Your fabric lanyard pouch is now complete! Slip in your keys, ID card, or other essentials, attach it to your lanyard, and enjoy hands-free convenience wherever you go.
