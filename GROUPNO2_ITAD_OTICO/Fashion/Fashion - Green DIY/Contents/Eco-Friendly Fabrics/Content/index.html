Eco-Friendly Fabrics

by: Heather Handmade




Make one of these great eco-friendly sewing projects that are reusable to help you save money and keep trash out of the landfill! There are a lot of reasons to use eco-friendly items in the kitchen and around the house. It helps the environment and it can help you safe money. I originally started using reusable items to help me save money, and it evolved into more than that. These are just a few of the items that our family uses and that I have created a tutorial for. Some of them are to keep your hands or clothes clean, some are for cleaning, some are for organizing or carrying, and some are to protect food.

Reusable Make Up Remover Pads

Create DIY reusable makeup remover pads to save money and reduce waste. This easy sewing project utilizes fabric scraps, providing a guilt-free solution for repurposing materials. Incorporating them into your nightly routine offers convenience, despite the need for regular washing.

General Tips:

  1. Fabric Choice: Opt for soft, absorbent materials like organic cotton, bamboo, or microfiber for gentle makeup removal.
  2. Size and Shape: Decide on dimensions, typically 3 to 4 inches in diameter for circles or squares.
  3. Layering: Consider adding multiple fabric layers for increased thickness and durability.
  4. Sewing Technique: Use a sewing machine or hand-stitching, leaving a small opening to turn the pad right side out. Backstitch at the beginning and end for security.
  5. Finishing Touches: Trim excess fabric, clip corners, and use a turning tool for a neat appearance.
  6. Pre-Washing: Launder pads before use to remove residues and ensure cleanliness.
  7. Storage: Keep pads in a clean container or pouch, possibly using a mesh laundry bag for washing.
  8. Care Instructions: Advise washing in a mesh bag on gentle cycle with mild detergent, air drying or tumble drying on low heat.

Things you will need:

  1. Fabric: Choose soft, absorbent fabric such as organic cotton, bamboo, or microfiber.
  2. Scissors: Use fabric scissors to cut out the desired shape and size of your pads.
  3. Thread: Select thread that matches your fabric or choose a contrasting color for decorative purposes.
  4. Sewing Machine or Needle: You'll need either a sewing machine or hand-sewing needle to stitch the layers of fabric together.
  5. Storage Container or Pouch: Choose a clean container or pouch to store your finished pads, keeping them organized and hygienic between uses.
