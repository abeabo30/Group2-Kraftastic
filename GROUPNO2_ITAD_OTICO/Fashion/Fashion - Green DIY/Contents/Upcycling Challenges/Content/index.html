Upcycling Challenges

by: WikiHow




Jeans are popular, and for good reason: they are comfy, fashionable, and go with just about everything. They don't last forever, however. Instead of throwing away your jeans when they get worn out, however, why not turn them into a trendy new bag? If you have an old button-up shirt that's on its way out too, you can use that to make the lining as well!

General Tips:

  1. Choose the Right Denim: Select a pair of jeans that are not too worn out. The denim should be sturdy enough to hold the shape of the purse. Consider the color and any existing patterns or embellishments that might enhance the look of your purse.
  2. Plan Your Design: Before cutting, decide on the size and shape of your purse. You might want to sketch your design or look up templates online. Think about whether you want a clutch, a tote, or a shoulder bag. This will help determine how you cut the fabric.
  3. Cutting the Fabric: Use sharp scissors for a clean cut. You can utilize different parts of the jeans for various features of your purse. For example, the back pockets can serve as external pockets on your purse, or the hem can become a decorative edge.
  4. Adding a Lining: Choose a fabric lining to add inside your denim purse to give it more structure and durability. The lining can also be a fun place to add a pop of color or a playful pattern.
  5. Incorporate Reinforcements: Depending on the design, you may need to reinforce the purse with interfacing or padding, especially if you're planning to carry heavier items. This helps the purse maintain its shape and supports the fabric.
  6. Sewing: If you have access to a sewing machine, use it to stitch your pieces together for durability. If you’re hand-stitching, use strong thread or embroidery floss for extra strength. Make sure to backstitch at the beginning and end of each seam to secure it.
  7. Add Closures and Straps: Consider how you will close your purse. Options include zippers, buttons, or magnetic snaps. For straps, you can repurpose the jean’s seams or belt loops, or you can use other materials like leather straps or woven fabrics.
  8. Personalize It: Add personal touches like appliqués, embroidery, studs, or patches. This is your chance to be creative and make the purse truly your own.
  9. Finishing Touches: Check all seams and finishes. Trim any loose threads, and ensure that everything is secure. You might want to wash the purse to ensure that all parts hold up well and to give it a finished look.
  10. Practical Considerations: Make sure your purse is practical for its intended use. If it’s for everyday use, ensure it has enough pockets and the right size to carry your essentials.

Things you will need:

  1. Old Jeans - Choose a pair that has a sturdy fabric and is in a color or wash you like.
  2. Fabric for Lining - Select a contrasting or complementary fabric to line your purse.
  3. Interfacing (optional) - For added structure, choose a suitable interfacing based on the desired stiffness.
  4. Closure Mechanisms - Consider zippers, magnetic snaps, or buttons, depending on your design.
  5. Straps Material - You can use the seam of the jeans, a belt, or purchase straps like leather or woven fabric.
  6. Thread - Use a strong thread that matches or contrasts with your denim for visual impact.
  7. Embellishments (optional) - Patches, embroidery floss, beads, studs, or any decorative items you'd like to add.
  8. Scissors - Sharp fabric scissors for cutting denim and other fabrics cleanly.
  9. Sewing Machine (optional but recommended) - A sewing machine that can handle thick fabrics like denim.
  10. Needles - Heavy-duty needles for both hand sewing and machine sewing.
  11. Ruler or Tape Measure - For measuring fabric and ensuring straight cuts.
  12. Pins or Clips - To hold fabric pieces together before and during sewing.
  13. Seam Ripper - In case of mistakes that need undoing.
  14. Chalk or Fabric Marker - For marking out cuts and sewing lines on the fabric.
  15. Iron and Ironing Board - For pressing the fabric, which is crucial for achieving crisp seams and professional-looking results.
