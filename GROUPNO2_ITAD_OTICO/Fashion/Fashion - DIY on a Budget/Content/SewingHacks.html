Sewing Hacks

by: Dainty Diaries




Sewing can be a fun and fulfilling hobby, especially when you learn some clever hacks that save time and reduce costs. Below, I'll share some sewing tips specifically for making a scrunchie and a DIY cat collar, which are both great projects for beginners and seasoned sewers alike.

Sewing Hacks for Making a Scrunchie:

  1. Use Fabric Scraps:
  • Scrunchies can be made from small pieces of fabric, making them perfect for using up leftovers from other projects. Look for fabrics with a bit of stretch like cotton jersey, satin, or velvet for a luxurious feel.
  1. Elastic Length Trick:
  • Instead of measuring the elastic for each scrunchie, use a standard size that fits most wrists comfortably (about 8-9 inches). You can adjust if needed, but this speeds up the process.
  1. Speedy Tube Turning:
  • After sewing the long edge of the fabric to create a tube, use a safety pin attached to one end of the fabric to quickly turn the tube right side out. Alternatively, attach a bodkin or a large needle to the end of the elastic to pull it through the tube.
  1. Quick-sew Method:
  • Instead of hand-sewing the final seam to close the scrunchie, you can use a small zigzag stitch on your sewing machine to join the ends of the tube. This is faster and often stronger than hand stitching.

Sewing Hacks for Making a DIY Cat Collar:

  1. Repurpose Materials:
  • Instead of buying new fabric, consider using old clothes, especially those made from durable materials like denim or canvas. You can also repurpose old belts or webbing for the collar base.
  1. Adjustable Design:
  • Use a slider buckle to make the collar adjustable. This not only ensures a better fit but also means you can make one size that fits most cats, simplifying the process if you're making multiple collars.
  1. Safety Feature:
  • Integrate a breakaway buckle for safety, which automatically opens under pressure. This is crucial for cat collars to prevent injury if the collar gets caught.
  1. Bell or ID Tag Loop:
  • Add a small loop of elastic or a fabric tab to the collar so you can attach a bell or an ID tag. This can be done by folding a small piece of elastic and sewing it in place when you're attaching the buckle.
  1. No-Sew Option:
  • For a completely no-sew collar, use a length of durable ribbon or nylon webbing and attach the ends using a fusible bonding web (iron-on hemming tape). Ensure to test the bond strength, especially if the collar includes a safety feature like a breakaway clasp.

General Cost-Saving and Time-Saving Tips:

  • Bulk Buying: Purchase materials like elastics, buckles, and fabric in bulk if you plan to make multiple items.
  • Multi-use Tools: Invest in tools that can be used for various crafts (like rotary cutters, durable scissors, and adjustable rulers).
  • Pre-Cut Materials: When possible, buy pre-cut materials to save time on cutting, especially for common sizes and shapes.
  • Template Use: Create reusable templates out of cardboard for commonly made items like collars, to speed up the cutting process.
