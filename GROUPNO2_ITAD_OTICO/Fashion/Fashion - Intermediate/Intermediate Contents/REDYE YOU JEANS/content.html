Re-dye your Jeans

by: Ashley Poskin

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Black Rit dye
  • Rubber gloves
  • Large pot or tub (find a non-food pot that you can designate for dyeing purposes)
  • Table salt or dye fixative
  • Wooden spoon (designated for dyeing)
  • Plastic drop cloth (a plastic tablecloth works well!)

Even if your jeans don’t look all that faded, a black dye bath helps fill in worn areas (usually found around the seams and pockets), making them look new again in just 30 minutes. The process can get a little messy, so it’s best to have access to a utility sink or a non-white porcelain sink. The dollar store is a great place to pick up your supplies (with the exception of the dye) because you don’t want to use anything that you’ll need for food prep later.

STEP 1: Fill a Pot With Hot Water and Dye

Fill a Pot With Hot Water and Dye - Fill a large pot with very hot water (not quite boiling), enough to fully submerge the jeans, and carefully empty the contents of the bottle of dye.

STEP 2: Add salt

Add salt - Add a ½ cup of salt and stir until dissolved. (Or follow the directions on the bottle if you’re using dye fixative.)

STEP 3: Dip your jeans into the dye

Dip your jeans into the dye Slowly dip the jeans into the pot, letting them absorb the water. Gently swirl the pants around in the pot, so the dye is evenly distributed

STEP 4: Soak for 30 minutes

Soak for 30 minutes - Let the jeans soak for up to 30 minutes, lift them from the water, and carefully wring out as much excess liquid as possible.

STEP 5: Rinse in cool water

Rinse in cool water - Transfer to an empty pot or plastic bin, and carry to the sink (a utility sink, if possible!) to remove any excess dye. Wash the jeans in cool water, kneading and wringing them out until the water runs clean.

STEP 6: Wash with detergent

Wash with detergent - Wash in cold water on gentle cycle with a small amount of liquid laundry detergent, and lay flat to dry. Continue to care for your jeans using these helpful tips and they’ll keep their color longer! (link to previous article)
